## The Line-Following Robot

After that success, I brought up another idea for the showcase. I showed them a project I had built for my own Robotics coursework — a line-following robot. Watching the video, they loved the idea and wanted to build their own.

But the project I had done was built with different hardware and assumed a level of complexity I couldn't just hand to them. I had to step back and think about how to make it theirs. We reduced the scope — stripped away the extras and kept the core. I adapted the sensors to what was available in the LEGO SPIKE Prime kit, and we worked through it together: the color sensor tracking a black line on white paper, red markers signaling where the track started and ended.

I also used this as an opportunity to introduce the scholars to a fundamental engineering concept — the **PID controller**. I kept it practical: how the robot could use proportional, integral, and derivative terms to follow the line smoothly rather than just jerking left and right. They didn't need to master the math, but they walked away understanding that there's a difference between something that works and something that's *tuned*.

From start to finish, we built everything ourselves — including the track. I walked them through laying out the course with black tape on white paper, placing the red markers at the start and end, and testing the robot against the real conditions it would face at the showcase.
